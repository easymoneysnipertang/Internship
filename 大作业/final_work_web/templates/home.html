About the story


Harry Potter is a series of fantasy novels written by British author J.K. Rowling. The series follows the life of a young wizard named Harry Potter and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, who all attend the Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The story begins with Harry discovering that he is a wizard and the famous "Boy Who Lived," having survived an attack by the dark wizard Lord Voldemort as a baby.
Throughout the series, Harry learns about his magical heritage, encounters various magical creatures and artifacts, and faces numerous challenges and adventures. He becomes involved in the ongoing battle between good and evil, with Voldemort and his followers, known as Death Eaters, seeking to gain power and control over the wizarding world.
The Harry Potter books explore themes of friendship, love, loyalty, and the struggle between good and evil. They have captivated readers of all ages and have become one of the most successful and beloved book series of all time. The series has also been adapted into a highly successful film franchise and has spawned various merchandise and spin-off works.

Film Series

The Harry Potter film series is a captivating journey through the wizarding world created by J.K. Rowling. Spanning eight movies, the series brings to life the magical adventures of Harry Potter, a young wizard with a destiny to fulfill.

Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry

Founded centuries ago, Hogwarts is steeped in tradition and is widely regarded as one of the finest magical schools. It is divided into four distinct houses: Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, and Slytherin. Each house has its own unique qualities and values, and students are sorted into these houses based on their traits and characteristics.

Gryffindor

Known for bravery and courage, Gryffindor values chivalry and daring. Its members are often noble and willing to stand up for what's right.

Slytherin

Slytherin is known for ambition and resourcefulness. They value cunning and determination, and are willing to do whatever it takes to achieve their goals.

Ravenclaw

Known for intelligence and wit, Ravenclaw values wisdom and creativity. Its members are often highly intellectual and enjoy pursuing academic pursuits.

Hufflepuff

Characterized by loyalty and hard work, Hufflepuff values dedication and fairness. They foster a strong sense of community and inclusiveness.
